Welcome to SABC CMMS Platform

A Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) is an intelligent platform designed to centralize, organize and optimize all maintenance operations within a production environment. It helps companies plan interventions, monitor equipment failures, manage spare parts and improve technician performance in real time. By collecting and analyzing operational data, the CMMS transforms maintenance into a smarter, faster and more reliable process. It reduces unplanned downtime, minimizes production losses and extends the lifespan of industrial equipment. As the control center of industrial maintenance, a CMMS helps companies build a more efficient, secure and productive production system.

Key Benefits of a CMMS:

1. Reduced Downtime

Prevent unexpected machine failures and minimize production interruptions.
A CMMS reduces downtime by helping maintenance teams detect, organize and resolve problems before they stop production.

How it reduces downtime:
  • Preventive Maintenance Scheduling

    The system automatically plans maintenance tasks before components fail.

  • Real-Time Failure Tracking

    Technicians can quickly identify recurring issues and react faster.

  • Spare Parts Availability

    The CMMS monitors inventory to ensure critical parts are available when needed.

  • Faster Interventions

    Work orders, maintenance history and technical documents are centralized, allowing technicians to solve problems more efficiently.

  • Machine Performance Monitoring

    Data analysis helps identify machines with frequent failures and prioritize maintenance actions.

  • Predictive Maintenance

    With enough operational data, the system can anticipate failures before they occur.


    • Result:
    Less unexpected downtime, fewer production interruptions and a more stable industrial process.

2. Preventive Maintenance Planning

Schedule maintenance tasks before equipment breaks down.
A CMMS helps companies schedule maintenance activities before equipment failures occur.

How preventive maintenance planning works:
  • Automatic Maintenance Scheduling

    The system creates maintenance tasks based on time, usage hours or production cycles.

  • Routine Inspections

    Regular inspections help detect wear, overheating or abnormal machine behavior early.

  • Maintenance Reminders

    Technicians receive alerts and notifications for upcoming interventions.

  • Reduced Equipment Failure

    Replacing critical components before they fail prevents unexpected breakdowns.

  • Better Maintenance Organization

    All maintenance plans, checklists and intervention histories are centralized in one platform.


    • result:
    Improved equipment reliability, fewer emergency repairs and longer machine lifespan.
